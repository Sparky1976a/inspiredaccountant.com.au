What Should I Bring to my Appointment?

“What do I bring?” is a frequent question, and having a list to refer to helps a lot of people. For this reason, I encourage you to print off this article, tick off the items that you have organised and also make a mental or written note as to where the various items can be found.

I also strongly suggest that you begin preparing your items as soon as you are able.  This will allow you a time buffer in case you need to chase up missing items. Many of these documents and particulars are not available on short notice.

Please come prepared to provide this information, in digital or paper form:

The Basics

  • Your name, address, date of birth and Tax File Number
  • Bank details for refund
  • Last year tax return

Income Information

  • Payment Summaries (PAYG, Termination)
  • Annual statements from managed investment funds
  • Annual bank statement (CSV preferred)
  • Annual share trading reports in PDF and CSV
  • Annual statement for investment properties
  • Annual private health insurance statement
  • Details of spouse taxable income
  • Details of foreign income earned
  • Dividend statements

Expense and Deduction Information

Please bring any expense statements or receipts you have for:

  • Insurances, e.g. Income Protection Insurance, Sickness and Accident
  • Investment property, depreciation report, mortgage bank statement
  • Accountant fees invoice for the previous year’s tax return
  • Vehicle log book and vehicle use (for work purposes)
  • Investment, Trust or Managed Fund
  • Tools, equipment and uniform
  • Work-related travel expenses
  • Education related expenses
  • Donations to charities

If you are unsure about any of the above, please give me a call or send me an email. Email is usually the most efficient.  I’ll endeavour to get back to you as fast as I can.
